# TikTok Script — Boring Goodbyes
**Topic:** The boring goodbye technique for reducing dog separation anxiety
**Concept:** Systematic desensitisation — making departures emotionally neutral
**Audience:** UK dog owners, 28–55, whose dogs show separation anxiety
**Length:** ~40 seconds read aloud
**Date created:** 2026-03-30

---

## SCRIPT

---

**[HOOK — 0–3 sec]**

The last thing you say to your dog before you leave the house is making their anxiety worse.

---

**[SETUP — 3–10 sec]**

I know. It feels kind. You say goodbye, you give them a cuddle, maybe a treat. You want them to feel loved before you go. [pause] But here's what's actually happening in your dog's brain.

---

**[INSIGHT — 10–30 sec]**

Dogs don't understand the word goodbye. What they understand is the emotional signal you send with it. A long farewell — eye contact, a high voice, a drawn-out moment — tells your dog that something significant is happening. [pause] And if something significant is happening when you leave, then leaving must be a threat worth worrying about.

This is what behavioural science calls a pre-departure cue. Your goodbye ritual is teaching your dog to start panicking before you've even closed the door.

[pause]

The fix is counterintuitive. You make leaving boring. No eye contact in the final two minutes. No drawn-out goodbye. You pick up your keys, you walk out, and you treat it like you're stepping into the next room. [pause] Over time, your dog's nervous system learns: departures are uneventful. There is nothing to fear here.

This is systematic desensitisation — and it is one of the first things we cover in the PAXA Solo 30-day protocol.

---

**[CTA — 30–45 sec]**

If your dog barks, paces, or destroys things when you leave, the problem isn't that they love you too much. It's that they've never been trained to feel safe alone. [pause] Link in bio for the full 30-day protocol.

## PRODUCTION NOTES

- **Tone:** Calm, direct, no uplift in voice on key words — authority, not enthusiasm
- **Pace:** Measured. Let the pauses land. Do not rush the insight section.
- **Visual suggestion:** Minimal — talking head or over-shoulder footage of a calm dog resting. No dramatic dog distress footage.
- **On-screen text overlays (optional):**
  - Hook line verbatim on screen at 0 sec
  - "Pre-departure cue" as a callout at the science moment
  - "Systematic desensitisation" on screen — spell it out, do not skip it
- **No music under voiceover** — or very low ambient only. The science needs to be heard clearly.
- **Caption:** Use the companion Instagram caption or write a short standalone caption per posting guidelines.
