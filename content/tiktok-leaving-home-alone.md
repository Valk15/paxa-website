## SCRIPT

---

**[HOOK — 0–3 sec]**

---

**[SETUP — 3–10 sec]**
Most owners do the same thing every morning.
Big fuss. Long goodbye. "Be good. I'll be back soon."
[pause]

---

**[INSIGHT — 10–30 sec]**
Here is what is actually happening.
Your dog does not understand your words.
But they read your energy perfectly.
[pause]
A drawn-out goodbye signals that something significant is about to happen.
It raises their cortisol — their stress hormone — before you have even left the room.
[pause]
Research in applied animal behaviour shows that pre-departure cues — your keys, your coat, your goodbye routine — are enough to trigger a full anxiety response on their own.
[pause]
So by the time the door closes, your dog is already in distress.
The problem started ten minutes ago.
[pause]
What actually works is the opposite of what feels natural.
No fuss. No eye contact. You leave like it means nothing.
Because to a dog with a secure attachment, it should mean nothing.
That security is trained — not assumed.
